CCTV (Closed-circuit television)

Closed-circuit television (CCTV), or video surveillance, involves the use of cameras to transmit signals to a limited number of monitors, distinguishing it from broadcast television. It is mainly used for security and monitoring in various environments, facilitating state surveillance and providing a safeguard worldwide. CCTV can operate continuously or selectively for specific events, and advanced systems with digital video recorders (DVRs) offer long-term recording and features like motion detection. Decentralized IP cameras have also emerged, enabling direct recording to network-attached storage or internal memory.

What Is a CCTV Camera? (Closed-circuit television Camera)

A closed-circuit television (CCTV) camera is a surveillance device that transmits video signals to specific monitors or recording devices, instead of broadcasting publicly.
